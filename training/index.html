How it works

  1. We talk. You tell me your team size, current Kubernetes exposure, and what you want to achieve — certification, production readiness, or both.
  2. Pre-training assessment. I run a diagnostic assessment before training begins. This isn't evaluative — it's to understand where your engineers are so I can pace the program correctly and focus on the right gaps.
  3. Instructor-led training. Live sessions combining lectures, real-time demonstrations, and guided hands-on labs. Mapped to CKA/CKAD/CKS exam domains. Typically 10 weeks, 2 days per week, 3 hours per session.
  4. Post-training assessment. A practical assessment at the end to measure progress and identify who is ready for certification. You get a full report with per-engineer results and readiness status.
  5. One month of post-training support. Questions, code reviews, and guidance as your team applies what they've learned in production.

What's covered

The curriculum follows the official CNCF CKA exam domains:

  • Module 1 — Cluster Architecture, Administration & Security
    kubeadm, HA control plane, RBAC, Helm, Kustomize, CRDs, operators
  • Module 2 — Networking
    Pod connectivity, Network Policies, Services, Ingress, Gateway API, CoreDNS
  • Module 3 — Workloads & Scheduling
    Deployments, rolling updates, ConfigMaps, Secrets, autoscaling, Pod scheduling
  • Module 4 — Storage
    Storage classes, dynamic provisioning, PersistentVolumes, PersistentVolumeClaims
  • Module 5 — Troubleshooting
    Cluster and node debugging, component failures, resource monitoring, networking issues
  • Module 6 — Exam Preparation
    Full review, exam-style labs, time management strategies, Q&A

Curriculum can be adjusted for CKAD or CKS focus depending on your team's goals.
